🧒 Where is your data stored? (Explain like you're 5)

Imagine you have a magic notebook.

Old way (localStorage): You had 2 notebooks — 1 on your PC and 1 on your iPhone. Writing in PC notebook didn't show in iPhone notebook. That's why it didn't sync.

New way (Firebase - what you have now): You have ONE magic notebook in the cloud (Google's big safe in Singapore). Your PC looks at it. Your iPhone looks at the SAME notebook. When you write "Do report" on PC, iPhone sees it instantly because it's the same notebook!

Is GitHub storing my tasks? No. GitHub only holds the empty notebook cover (the code). The writings (your tasks) go to the magic cloud notebook (Firebase). Guards check your email login, only you can open it.

What if no internet? The notebook makes a photo copy on your device, so you can still read. When internet back, it syncs.
